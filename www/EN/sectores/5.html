The sea, always the sea

One of the popular Menorca songs says that the island sounds constantly to the song of the sea and the wind. Menorca can’t be understood without the Tramontana and the beaches. The sea has been generous with a piece of land shaping 216 kilometres of coast which hides contrasting beauty spots of different hues and colours.

In the south the bays and coves offer the visitor fine white sand lapped by crystal turquoise waters. Many of these are at the mouth of the “barrancos” the valleys that cross the island and leave a soft, gentle landscape. Idyllic scenery in which to enjoy the sea, the breeze and the sun. Without worries, without stress. Relax in the sea, bask in the sun, and simply enjoy.

The north coast is more savage. Carved by the force of the wind and waves, the coves combine the turquoise water with the copper brown of clay. These beaches are well known for their great beauty, where one can enter in symbiosis with the unspoilt scenery.

The Menorcans don’t understand life without the sea. They understand how to enjoy the fruits of the Mediterranean with discretion and care. They don’t lose an opportunity to have a red prawn from the island, to try a sublime scorpion fish, or a divine lobster, the star of Menorcan gastronomy.

And if you are a lover of diving, submerge yourself and marvel and the marine life in Menorca, it won’t disappoint you.
